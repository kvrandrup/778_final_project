Background and Intention

With this project, I hope to highlight the disparity in access to green space and parks within King County as it related to population demographics, especially in terms of gender and race.

King County has 846.63 sq mi of park and 1,810.93 miles of trail, in and out of the city. The access to these parks and trails should be, theoretically, reflective of the overall makeup of the county; King County is 69.5% white and 50.2% female. This is an even gender split and a higher racial diversity than most other counties in Washington.

Based on personal experience, I know the representation in these spaces is not representative of the overall population. I hope for this project to be informative to outdoor enthusiasts and city planners within King County. Outlining the disparity is the first step towards making the outdoors a more equitable space.
